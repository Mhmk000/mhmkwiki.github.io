Technical Manuals: Writing and Formats

MACRO-TOPIC: Professional English

What is a manual?

A manual is a document that provides instructions or guidelines on how to perform an activity and serves as a reference book on that activity.

  • Policy manuals: rules and principles of an organization.
  • Procedure manuals: how a specific work activity is performed.
  • User manuals: guidance for the end-user of a product.

Structure of a Manual

A manual is organized into three general components:

  • Front matter: Title page, table of contents, and safety warnings.
  • Body: The core section with step-by-step instructions.
  • Back matter: Glossary, index, and troubleshooting sections.

Print vs. E-Manuals

Choosing the right format depends on the user's needs:

  • Print Manuals: Reliable (no power needed) and rugged, but expensive and bad for the environment.
  • E-Manuals: Economical, portable, and easy to search, but vulnerable to being copied or losing power.

The Writing Process

To write an effective manual, follow these steps:

  1. Preparation: Understand the procedure thoroughly before typing.
  2. Writing & Testing: Draft the content and verify it with actual users to see if it makes sense.
  3. Internationalization: Limit idioms and use metric units for a global audience.
